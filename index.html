      • Proposal / Offer
        • When one person signifies to another his willingness to do or abstain from doing anything with a view of obtaining assent of another to such act or abstinence , he is said to make a proposal"
        • Essentials
          • Can be positive negative
          • with a view of obtaining assent
        • Classification
          • General Offer
            • made to public, anyone can accept and do the act
          • Special / Specific offer
            • made to specific or ascertained person
          • Cross offer
            • Two parties exchange identical offers without knowledge of each other, is not acceptance
          • Counter offer
            • Counter offer = qualified acceptance subject to modifications and variables in terms of original offer
          • Standing or continuing or open offer
            • allowed to remain open for a period of time
        • Essentials of valid offer
          • capable of creating legal relations
            • Social invitation not offer
          • certain, definite and vague
          • communicated to the offeree
            • must be comms, otherwise no acceptance
          • made with a view of obtaining consent
            • not a view of disclosing the intention of making an offer
          • may be conditional
          • Should not contain a term the non compliance of which would amount to acceptance
          • Either specific or general
          • Different from mere statement of intention, an invitation to offer, a mere communication of information, a prospectus or advertisement
          • Express or implied
          • Statement of price not offer
        • Acceptance
          • Legal rules regarding acceptance
            • Given only by the person to whom offer is made
            • Absolute and unqualified
            • Communicated
            • Prescribed mode only
            • Time
              • Within specified time limit
              • reasonable time
            • Mere silence is not acceptance
            • Acceptance by conduct/ Implied acceptance
        • Communication of offer and acceptance
          • Offer
            • Complete when it comes to knowledge of offeree
              • When a person reads the letter of offer
          • Acceptance
              • As against the Proposer (receiver)
                • When put into course of transmission
              • As against the Acceptor (sender)
                • When received by proposer
          • Revocation
            • As against the sender
              • When it is out of his hands / Put into transmission
            • As against the receiver
              • When it comes to his knowledge
          • Proposal can be revoked
            • Any time before the communication of ITS ACCEPTANCE is complete as against the proposer (Receiver)
          • Acceptance may be revoked
            • Any time before the communication of acceptance is complete as against the acceptor
              • Before received by the proposer
          • Modes of revocation of offer
            • By notice of revocation
            • By lapse of time
            • By non fulfillment of condition precedent
            • By death or insanity
            • By counter offer
            • By non acceptance according to the prescribed or usual mode
            • By subsequent illegality

        • must either perform or offer to perform, their respective promises unless such performance is dispensed with or excused under thr povisions
        • Parties may bind the representatives if actual party dies
      • By whom a contract may be performed
        • Depends on the nature of the contract, it may be performed either only by the actual promisor or any other person
      • Effect of accepting performance from third person
        • He cannot afterwards enforce it against the promisor
        • Even if the promisor hasn't allowed or ratified the act of 3rd partyy
      • Joint Promisors
        • It must be performed jointly by the actual promisors or their legal representatives
      • Distinction b/w Succession and Assignment
        • Assignment
          • Assign benefits of a contract to a third party but cannot assign the liabilities thereunder
        • Succession
          • Through the legal process of "Heir" thingy
          • Both liabilities and benefits
      • Effect of refusal to accept offer of performance
        • Promisor is not responsible for non performace
        • Doesn't lose his rights under the contract
        • Conditions
          • Offer must be unconditional
          • proper time and place
          • reasonable opportunity for promisee to examine the thing being delivered or ascertain whether it is the intention of the promisor to perform his promise wholly
      • Effect of refusal to perform promise
        • Promisee has the rights
          • to terminate the contract
          • to indicate by words, conduct that he is interested in its continuance
          • Entitled to claim damages due to such breach

        • Where no application is not be made and no time is specified

        • Application for performance on certain day to be at proper time and place

        • occuring before the time fixed for performance has arrived.
        • Effects
          • Treat the contract as rescinded and sue the other party for damages
          • Or wait till due date to do so
            • In such a time the guilty party may still perform their part
            • They may take advantage of the contract being discharged due to any supervening impossibility
      • Actual Breach of Contract
      • Remedies for breach of contract
        • Suit for damages
          • Compensation for loss or damage
            • Caused by natural causes
            • Or parties knew when they made contract
            • Cannot be given for any indirect damages
          • Compensation for failure to discharge liability resembling those created by contract
          • Remedy by way of damages or kind of damages
            • General / Ordinary
            • Special
              • Brought to the notice of other party
              • Any special damages can be claimed
            • Vindictive or Exemplary
              • Vindictive - In case of promise to marry, injury to feelings
              • Exemplary
                • In case a customer's cheque is dishonored wrongfully by banker
                • Damages the goodwill and reputation of the banker
            • Nominal
              • Not incurred any real damages so something is awarded just to put it on paper
            • Damages for deterioration caused by delay
              • Deterioration damages can be claimed without any pre-notice to the carrier
              • Deterioration also means loss of special opportunity for sale
            • Pre-fixed damages
              • Pre-fixed damages and actual damages
                • Whichever is lower
          • Penalty and liquidated damages
            • Penalty means any unreasonable amount just to punish the party for not performing their promise
            • Liquidated damages means pre-fixed
            • No distinction b/w these in Indian Law
              • They provide reasonable compensation not exceeding the Liquidated damages
        • Rescission of contract
          • The other party may treat the contract as rescinded
        • Quantum Meruit
          • "As much as the party doing the service has deserved"
          • In breach of contract, one party has already performed PART of their promise and wish to be compensated for it
          • They can do so under Quantum Meruit
          • Cases
            • Agreement discovered to be void or contract becomes void
            • Something is done without any intention to do it gratuitously
            • Contract to render services but nothing about renumeration
            • When one party abandons or refuses to perform contract
            • contract is divisible and the party not in default has enjoyed the benefit of part performance
        • Suit for performance
          • Court may order the party to carry out their promise
        • Suit for injunction
          • The court may restrain him from doing what he promised not to do
        • Party rightfully rescinding a contract is entitled to compensation
    • Contingent Contracts
      • Essentials
        • Performance would depend upon the happening or non-happening of some event or condition
        • collateral to the contract
          • Not part of contract
        • Contingent event should not be mere 'will' of the promisor
          • It can be in hands of the promisor but there must be some kind of uncertainity
        • Event must be uncertain
      • Rules relating to enforcement
        • Cannot be enforced until and unless that event happens
          • if happening becomes impossible, contract becomes void
        • Enforcement of contracts contingent on an event not happening
          • Only enforceable when happening becomes impossible
        • Contract would cease to enforceable when it is contingent upon the conduct of a living person and that living person does something which renders such "conduct" as impossible
          • B agrees to marry C when her father dies. She (B) marries someone else when her father is alive, thus her marrying C is impossible so. C can sue her for breach of contract
        • Contingent on happening of specified event within the fixed time
          • Becomes void if at the expiration of time , such event has'nt happened
        • Contingent on specified event not happening within the fixed time
          • may be enforced by law when such time expires without the even thappening
        • Contingent on an impossible event
          • Void, whether the impossibility of the event is known or not to the parties to the agrement
